Graduate student of the dual program MA/Ph.D. Linguistics/Romance Language at the University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill. I am interested in the acquisition of a second language: from brain processing to phonetic production and the implementation of phonetic tools to assess second language proficiency. 

Background

I am a Medical Doctor graduated in Mexico, from the Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México (UNAM), with a specialty in Audiology and Phoniatry from the General Hospital of México. After thirteen years practicing my specialty in hospital settings in Mexico City, in the General Hospital of Mexico (Hospital General de México) and in the Pemex Centralized South Specialty Hospital (Hospital Central Sur de Alta Especialidad), due to family reasons, I moved to Spain first, and then to the United States. In my daily clinical practice, I was familiar with the neurological substrate of language and the devastating consequences that hearing impairment can have in language development. 
Language has been present in many of my professional activities: as the editor of ¿Qué Pasa? The Panamerican Society of Audiology’s newsletter, as part of the translating team of the International Journal of Audiology, translating and clinically validating diagnostic tools from English to Spanish, and also by getting closer to literary use of language.
